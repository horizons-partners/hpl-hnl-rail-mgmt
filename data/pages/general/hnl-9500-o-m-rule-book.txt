===== 1 Scope =====

The purpose of this Operations and Maintenance (O&M) Rule Book is to serve as guidance for the Honolulu Rail Transit Project (HRTP). These rules apply to all Hitachi Rail Honolulu (HRH) Personnel and contractors during the Operations and Maintenance of the HRTP. This document will become valid in defined operational sections of the HRTP as sections are progressively opened for Trial Running and revenue service. The Rule Book will be distributed electronically to all personnel. All Personnel must be conversant with these rules.

\\ 
==== 1.1 Purpose ====

The intent of these rules is to govern and assist Personnel in performing their duties safely and effectively. Each Personnel is responsible to learn and understand the rules and maintain a thorough knowledge of all procedures specific to individual work assignments. The O&M Rule Book has been developed for the purpose of providing general rules for the organization that will be supported by the system plans, procedures, work instructions, forms, and templates to ensure safe operations and maintenance of the rail transit system (RTS).
\\ 

==== 1.2 Applicability ====

This document is applicable to all HRH Personnel and contractors. Upon issuance all Hitachi Rail Honolulu Personnel must carry the Rule Book while on duty and be able to present it for inspection when requested by management personnel.

===== 2 General Operating Rules =====

\\ 

==== 2.1 Safety Priority ====

  * Safety is always the first consideration

  * Take care of your own safety and that of others who may be affected by your actions at work

  * Ensure the safety of staff members, contractors, passengers, and visitors and provide a safe environment of HRH for everyone

\\ 

==== 2.2 Safety Policy ====

The Rail Safety Policy is:

  * Seek to achieve zero harm to people in all operations

  * Promote a positive safety culture and commitment from staff

  * Comply with all relevant statutory regulations of the State of Hawaii

  * Identify railway hazards and deploy mitigations to control risk exposure in accordance with risk levels 

  * Commit adequate resources to establish a safe operating environment

  * Systematically manage and communicate on rail safety matters 

  * Rigorously audit and review the safety implications of all activities 

  * Consult staff and encourage active participation at all levels 

  * Seek continuous improvement by learning and benefiting from own experience and the experiences of others 

  * Cooperate fully with all stakeholders with shared responsibility 

\\

**Indication: Stop**

o Reduce Speed

{{:general:hnl-09500-f02.png?nolink|}}

{{:general:hnl-09500-f03.png?nolink|}}

**Indication: Move forward.**

o **Move Backwards**

{{:general:hnl-09500-f04.png?nolink|}}

Aspect: Move hand, flag, or light vertically in a circle at half arm’s length across the body below the shoulder.

**Indication: Move Backwards.**

{| border="0" style="border:0px solid #ddd;vertical-align:top;" title=""
|+ style="color:black" |

“0” Stop and remain standing

“00” Before moving forward and to acknowledge a hand signal

“000” Before moving in reverse (backing up)

“0000” Request for signal to be given or repeated if not understood.
